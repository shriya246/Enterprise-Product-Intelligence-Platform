# Personas

## Product Manager — "Priya," primary persona

**Role:** Owns a product area at a Series A–C startup or a mid-size product team. Reports to a VP Product or CEO.

**Goals:** Make defensible prioritization calls quickly. Spend more time talking to customers and less time exporting CSVs between tools. Walk into a roadmap review with data-backed answers, not gut feel.

**Pain today:** Retention data lives in Amplitude, feedback lives in a spreadsheet or Zendesk, roadmap lives in Productboard or Notion, and none of the three talk to each other. Answering "why did activation drop last week" means three tabs and a Slack thread with an analyst.

**What PulseAI gives them:** One workspace where the AI assistant can answer "why is retention dropping" by looking at the same events and feedback Priya would have had to manually correlate. The roadmap module surfaces which backlog items have the strongest engagement + feedback signal behind them.

**Success looks like:** Priya can answer a stakeholder's question in the meeting, not "let me get back to you."

## Product Analyst — "Marcus"

**Role:** Embedded or central analytics function supporting several PMs.

**Goals:** Direct access to raw event data, not just pre-built dashboards. Needs to build custom cohorts and funnels without waiting on an engineer to write a query.

**Pain today:** Analytics tools gate advanced segmentation behind expensive tiers, or require SQL access that PMs don't have and that Marcus becomes the bottleneck for.

**What PulseAI gives them:** A funnel builder and cohort analysis view built directly on the org's event table, with row-level security so each org only ever sees its own data — no separate warehouse to manage.

**Success looks like:** Marcus builds a new cohort or funnel in minutes without writing SQL, and PMs stop pinging him for one-off pulls.

## UX Researcher — "Dana"

**Role:** Runs qualitative research — interviews, surveys, support ticket review — and turns it into synthesized themes for the product team.

**Goals:** Turn hundreds of loose pieces of feedback (support tickets, survey verbatims, App Store reviews) into a handful of clear themes with sentiment, fast.

**Pain today:** Manual tagging and clustering in a spreadsheet doesn't scale past a few dozen entries, and dedicated research-ops tools are their own separate paid subscription.

**What PulseAI gives them:** CSV import of raw feedback, automatic theme clustering and sentiment tagging via the AI layer, so Dana starts from a synthesized view instead of a raw pile.

**Success looks like:** Dana imports a batch of 200 support tickets and has a themed, sentiment-tagged summary in under a minute instead of a day of manual coding.

## Engineering Manager — "Alex"

**Role:** Owns delivery for one or more squads; sits in sprint planning and roadmap reviews.

**Goals:** Know whether a shipped feature is actually being used before committing more engineering time to it. Push back on scope with adoption data, not opinion.

**Pain today:** Feature adoption data, if it exists at all, lives in a dashboard Alex doesn't have access to or doesn't think to check before a planning meeting.

**What PulseAI gives them:** A feature adoption view tied directly to the `features` registry, so "is anyone using the thing we shipped in Q2" has a one-click answer.

**Success looks like:** Alex brings adoption data into a planning conversation unprompted, because checking it became part of his routine.

## Executive — "Sam," CEO or VP Product

**Role:** Needs the state of the product business at a glance, not a tool to operate.

**Goals:** A single screen that answers "are we healthy" — DAU/WAU/MAU trend, retention, adoption of recent bets — without needing to learn an analytics tool.

**Pain today:** Getting a board-ready product snapshot means asking a PM to compile it manually from three tools, usually the night before the meeting.

**What PulseAI gives them:** An executive dashboard that rolls up the KPIs that matter into one screen, always current, no compilation required.

**Success looks like:** Sam opens PulseAI five minutes before a board meeting and has everything needed, with no Slack message to a PM required.
